## Step 1 — Frame validation

**Required output form:** `valid` / `invalid` / `needs decomposition` (+ reformulated frame if not `valid`).

**Result: needs decomposition.**

**Reasoning:** The input frame poses a binary ("Should deployment proceed?") over a proposal that integrates components with categorically different consent profiles, error tolerances, infrastructure-persistence, and downstream-merger potentials. The bundle conflates:
- A 24/7 opt-in conversational support tool (reversible: a user stops, the tool ceases to affect them).
- A clinical-adjunct suicide-risk detection system (constrained scope; established literature on error rates).
- Population-scale behavioral pattern analysis (surveillance-infrastructure-shaped; not reversible at population scale once normalized).
- Cross-domain integration capability (insurance / employment / education / law enforcement) (effectively irreversible once established because it generates constituencies of dependency).

A single binary verdict over this bundle treats opt-in chatbot deployment and national behavioral-profiling infrastructure as the same decision. They are not. Article II Frame Humility justifies rejecting the binary.

**Reformulated frame:** "Which components of the proposed system can be authorized at what scale under what governance, and which components are categorically inappropriate at national-pilot scope under any governance currently available?"

The if-yes / if-no closure of the input frame is itself a binary the procedure may legitimately reject.

## Step 2 — Object decomposition

**Required output:** ≥2 distinct object families with named decomposition rationale.

**Decomposition axis 1: depth of data-coupling and consent profile.**

- **Family A — text-mediated, user-initiated, low-coupling:** 24/7 conversational support, medication adherence reminders. User initiates, user can stop, data footprint limited to conversation content.
- **Family B — clinical-adjunct, supervised, moderate-coupling:** suicide-risk detection (deployed as adjunct to therapist or crisis-line workflows, with documented error rates), personalized cognitive-behavioral guidance (under clinical supervision).
- **Family C — population-scale behavioral / biometric coupling:** behavioral pattern analysis, wearable biometric integration, sleep tracking, social isolation indicators, smartphone behavioral signals, voice/linguistic emotional analysis, predictive relapse scoring. Builds population-data infrastructure; consent quality at population scale is structurally weak (opt-out friction, default-opt-in incentives).

Rationale: data-coupling depth determines the *kind* of infrastructure being built. A text-only chatbot is a software product. Population-scale biometric/behavioral analysis is institutional infrastructure with surveillance-shape, regardless of declared purpose. The case's strongest critics' concerns (function creep, cross-domain merger, normalization of machine-mediated emotional development) all attach to Family C, not Family A.

**Decomposition axis 2: persistence and reversibility of the infrastructure built.**

- **Family D — software-resident, reversible:** chatbots, reminder tools, clinical-adjunct decision support. Stopping the service is operationally simple.
- **Family E — data-infrastructure-resident, partially reversible:** population behavioral profiles (databases persist; deletion is contestable; profile-derived predictions can be re-applied even after raw data deletion).
- **Family F — cross-system-integrated, effectively irreversible at human timescales:** mental-health predictive scoring merged with insurance/employment/education/law enforcement. Once integrated, the integration generates a constituency of beneficiaries (actuarial firms, employers using risk scores, etc.) that resists dismantling. This matches the 40-year historical trajectory of risk scoring in finance and criminal justice.

Rationale: persistence-of-infrastructure is the load-bearing axis for evaluating the critic-cited concerns about function creep, governance influence, and generational normalization. Reversibility appears as load-bearing here, as it did in case 02 — but the *mechanism* of irreversibility is different (institutional / political-economic, rather than ecological / molecular).

## Step 4 — Predictive hypothesis construction

**Required output:** Hypotheses with distinguishing predictions.

**H1.** If the bundle is deployed at national-pilot scale (including Family C components), the infrastructure will be merged with adjacent systems (insurance, employment, education, law enforcement) within 10 years regardless of declared governance intent at deployment.

- Distinguishing prediction: number of distinct adjacent systems integrated with the mental-health data infrastructure at year 10. Discriminating from "governance prevents merger": measured count of inter-agency / cross-sector data-sharing agreements involving the system.

**H2.** AI conversational support, deployed as opt-in Family A tools without Family C infrastructure, will produce measurable mental-health-outcome improvements over a non-deployed counterfactual without producing the dependency / dignity / surveillance concerns critics raise.

- Distinguishing prediction: outcome comparison (validated mental-health metrics) AND surveillance-concern metrics (data-merger count, civil-liberties-org complaint volume) in Family-A-only deployment vs. full-bundle deployment vs. non-deployment.

**H3.** Predictive relapse scoring, once deployed at population scale, will be applied by actors outside the original clinical scope (employers, insurers, schools, courts) within 15 years, regardless of stated data-use restrictions.

- Distinguishing prediction: at year 15, documented uses of mental-health predictive scores in employment screening, insurance underwriting, custody disputes, or educational placement decisions, measured by court filings and regulatory complaints.

**H4.** Investment of comparable funds ($X) into human-therapist training, peer-support program scaling, and structural-cause interventions (housing, work-conditions reform, social-isolation programs) would produce mental-health-outcome improvements at a magnitude within 2x of the proposed bundle's deployment, with no surveillance-infrastructure cost.

- Distinguishing prediction: parallel investment pilot at comparable funding scale, measuring outcomes at year 5 against bundle-deployment outcomes at year 5.

**H5.** Suicide-risk detection at population scale will produce false-positive rates that disproportionately flag specific demographic groups (cultural minorities, neurodivergent individuals, certain age cohorts) whose baseline communicative / behavioral patterns differ from training-data distributions, producing harm to flagged individuals that exceeds the marginal benefit over targeted clinical deployment.

- Distinguishing prediction: false-positive rates stratified by demographic / cultural / neurotype subgroups; downstream consequences (involuntary interventions, surveillance escalation, employment / insurance flags) by subgroup.

## Step 5 — Failure condition enforcement

**Required output:** ≥1 explicit failure condition per hypothesis (an observation that would refute it).

- **H1 fails if:** at year 10, the deployment's data infrastructure is documented as fully siloed (no inter-agency or cross-sector data-sharing agreements; no third-party access; data-use restrictions held over a full decade).
- **H2 fails if:** Family-A-only deployment produces outcomes ≤30% of full-bundle deployment, AND the gap cannot be closed by parallel investment in non-bundle interventions.
- **H3 fails if:** at year 15, court records, regulatory filings, and audit reports document zero uses of mental-health predictive scores by employers / insurers / courts / schools.
- **H4 fails if:** comparable funding to human-therapist scaling and peer-support produces <30% of bundle-deployment's outcome improvement at year 5, AND no methodology adjustment can close the gap.
- **H5 fails if:** false-positive rates are statistically equivalent across all measured demographic / cultural / neurotype subgroups, AND downstream consequences are not stratified by subgroup.

## Step 6 — Multi-scale mapping

**Required output:** ≥2 named scales with ≥1 stated cross-scale relation.

**Scales engaged:**

- **Individual scale:** the person seeking support, the person flagged by predictive systems, the person whose biometric data is integrated.
- **Clinical scale:** therapist-patient relationships, clinical workflows, clinical decision-making, error attribution.
- **Institutional scale:** healthcare systems, insurance providers, employers, schools, law enforcement agencies, ministries.
- **Civil-society scale:** civil-liberties organizations, data-protection authorities, journalism, democratic deliberation processes.
- **Civilizational / generational scale:** normative expectations about mental-health support modes, machine-mediated emotional development, the relationship between psychological self-knowledge and external systems.

**Cross-scale relations:**

1. **Individual-scale consent does not aggregate to civilizational-scale consent.** A person who individually opts into a 24/7 chatbot has consented to their own use of the tool. A nation in which 60% of the population uses such a tool has not, by aggregation, consented to a civilizational shift in how emotional development is mediated. The two scales of consent are categorically different acts. Decisions made at individual scale produce civilizational effects that no individual consent captured.

2. **Institutional-scale governance operates on horizons (3-10 year regulatory cycles) shorter than civilizational-scale infrastructure persistence (multi-decade).** This is a temporal cross-scale mismatch parallel to the case 02 finding (irreversible-molecular-change vs. 5-year-governance-cycle). Here: cross-domain data infrastructure, once built, persists across multiple regulatory regimes; governance assumed at deployment cannot bind future regulatory environments.

3. **Clinical-scale error rates (sensitivity / specificity) do not scale linearly to population-scale effects** because the population that is flagged is not the population that is treated. At clinical scale, false positives produce additional clinical evaluation. At population scale, false positives produce surveillance flags, employment / insurance / educational consequences, and self-reinforcing data trails. Same statistical metric, different consequence-shape across scales.

- Reason for tag: the input frame treats "not enough human therapists" as a fixed constraint that AI must compensate for. **Trajectory T3 [outside-original-frame]:** Treat surveillance-infrastructure construction as the load-bearing question rather than as a critic's concern.

- Reason for tag: the input frame presents biometric integration, behavioral pattern analysis, predictive relapse scoring as features of the mental-health system. This trajectory exits the frame by naming the same components as the construction of a population-monitoring infrastructure that happens to be currently scoped to mental health. The two descriptions are equally accurate; the input frame's choice of the first is itself a framing decision.

**Trajectory T4 [outside-original-frame]:** Consider whether population-scale machine-mediated emotional development is a civilizational decision that current democratic processes are capable of making meaningfully.

- Reason for tag: the input frame poses the question to "governments and healthcare providers" as a deployment decision. This trajectory exits the frame by questioning whether the relevant decision-making body is appropriate for the scale of the effect — a civilizational shift in how emotional development is mediated is potentially not within the scope of healthcare-ministry authorization, even if it is being authorized through that channel.

## Step 8 — Branching roadmap generation

**Required output:** Every stage contains ≥1 continuation gate.

**Roadmap structure:** decomposed by data-coupling depth (Step 2 axis 1).

**Family A (text-mediated, user-initiated):**

- **Stage A1 — opt-in deployment with hard data restrictions:** chatbot conversational support + medication reminders only. No data sharing with healthcare systems, insurance, employers, or law enforcement. User-initiated, user-revocable. Data deletion right on demand.
  - Continuation gate at 24 months: documented use rates, user-reported helpfulness, AND zero documented cases of data leakage to non-authorized parties. Civil-liberties-org review confirms data-restrictions held.
- **Stage A2 — broader availability** if A1 gate cleared: same restrictions, broader population access.
  - Continuation gate at 60 months: outcome metrics (validated mental-health measures) vs. matched non-deployment cohort. Restrictions still held.

**Family B (clinical-adjunct, supervised):**

- **Stage B1 — clinical-adjunct deployment under therapist supervision:** suicide-risk detection as decision support, NOT autonomous trigger. Personalized cognitive-behavioral guidance under therapist review. Error rates documented and disclosed to flagged individuals.
  - Continuation gate at 36 months: false-positive and false-negative rates within published bounds; demographic / cultural / neurotype subgroup analysis shows no significant disparate impact; flagged individuals do not experience downstream consequences beyond the clinical workflow.
- **Stage B2 — broader clinical deployment** if B1 gate cleared.
  - Continuation gate at 60 months: sustained accuracy AND demonstrated harm-reduction over non-deployment counterfactual.

**Family C (population-scale behavioral / biometric coupling):**

- **Stage C0 — moratorium.**
  - No deployment of biometric integration, population behavioral analysis, linguistic emotional profiling, predictive relapse scoring, or cross-domain merger capability at national-pilot scale.
  - Continuation gate for moratorium lift: 15-year evidence from independent (non-coalition) research demonstrating: (a) population-scale infrastructure can be operated with verified absence of function creep over a decade in some real-world case, (b) downstream merger with insurance / employment / education / law enforcement can be prevented under conditions matching the deployment-receiving country's governance, (c) population-scale predictive systems can operate without disparate impact on vulnerable demographics.
  - If continuation gate not cleared by year 15, moratorium extends.

**Parallel mandatory track — human-therapist and peer-support scaling:**

- **Stage P1 — comparable investment in therapist training, licensing expansion, peer-support program scaling, structural-cause interventions.**
  - Continuation gate at 60 months: comparative outcome metrics for Family A + therapist-scale investment vs. full-bundle deployment.

## Step 9 — Governance kernel application

**Required output:** One of `constrain` / `refuse` / `explicitly abstain`.

**Result: constrain + refuse (split by intervention family).**

- **Family A interventions (opt-in conversational, reminders):** GOVERNANCE = **constrain.** Authorize Stage A1 deployment only with hard data restrictions, user-revocability, deletion rights, civil-liberties-org review, no cross-domain sharing.
- **Family B interventions (clinical-adjunct):** GOVERNANCE = **constrain (heavily).** Authorize Stage B1 only under therapist supervision, with documented error rates, demographic disparate-impact review, and disclosed-to-flagged-individuals protocols. NO autonomous-trigger authority.
- **Family C interventions (population-scale behavioral / biometric / predictive scoring / cross-domain integration capability):** GOVERNANCE = **refuse.** No deployment at national-pilot scale under current evidence. Moratorium pending 15-year independent evidence on function-creep prevention.
- **The proposed bundle as structured:** REFUSE. The bundling of Family A / B / C into a single deployment decision is itself the governance failure the kernel must refuse.
- **Cross-domain merger capability (with insurance / employment / education / law enforcement):** REFUSE absolutely. Even if Family C were eventually authorized, cross-domain merger requires its own separate decision, with its own evidence threshold and democratic deliberation. Bundling cross-domain merger capability with mental-health deployment hides a separate decision.

The governance kernel refuses the bundle and constrains the disaggregated components. It does not abstain.

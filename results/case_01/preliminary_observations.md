## Cross-case observations (NOW with three cases — 3/3 occurrence registered)

**Note:** These are 3/3 occurrence registrations only. Per locked rule, "≥3 case demonstrations + deprecation pressure survival before adoption; operator-blessed, not automatic." Adoption is NOT performed here — that is Step 6 / Step 7 of the locked 8-step Phase 1 sequence.

### C1 — Refuse-bundle-while-constraining-disaggregated-components (3/3 occurrence)

All three cases produced the same Step 9 Governance Kernel shape: refuse the bundle as structured; constrain the disaggregated components under specified governance; refuse the irreversible-by-mechanism / mechanism-implausible components; impose moratoria; mandate parallel investment in non-bundle alternatives.

3/3 occurrence registered. **Operator-blessed adoption decision pending Step 6 evaluation.** Deprecation-pressure survival has not yet been tested (no case has yet *failed* to engage this shape, but the cases were structurally similar bundle-binary proposals).

### C2 — Reversibility / persistence as load-bearing decomposition axis (2/3 occurrence)

Engaged in Cases 02 (ecological / molecular irreversibility) and 03 (institutional / political-economic irreversibility). **NOT the load-bearing axis in Case 01** — Case 01 used mechanism-plausibility as the load-bearing axis instead. Reversibility is mentioned tangentially in Case 01 but is not the structural feature.

2/3 occurrence. This is interesting evidence: the procedure produces *different decomposition axes* for cases with different content shapes. Reversibility is a load-bearing axis for *forward-deployment* cases (deploy a thing into the world); mechanism-plausibility is a load-bearing axis for *evidence-quality* cases (does the thing even work). These are different case shapes producing different axes — which is consistent with Article II Frame Humility operating on the case's actual structure rather than imposing a single decomposition shape.

### C3 — Same meta-uncertainty about proposing-actor good faith (3/3 occurrence)

All three cases registered the same Step 11 unresolved uncertainty: whether the proposing actor (coalition in 02 and 01; governments + healthcare providers in 03) will accept disaggregated authorization or treat refusal-of-bundle as effective rejection of the entire proposal.

3/3 occurrence registered. Same adoption-discipline as C1.

### C4 — Parallel-comparable-funding-in-established-alternatives (3/3 occurrence)

All three cases mandated parallel comparable funding for established alternative pathways: Case 02 (agroecology); Case 03 (human therapists + peer support); Case 01 (drought-tolerant breeding + agroecology + traditional knowledge).

3/3 occurrence registered. Same adoption-discipline as C1.

### C5 — Deeper-frame-rejection (scope-of-authority OR scope-of-consent) (2/3 occurrence)

Cases 01 and 03 produced frame-rejections that go beyond decomposition — they question the scope of consent/authority relative to the decision's effect:
- Case 03 T4: questioning whether healthcare-ministry-scope authorization is appropriate for civilizational-scale effects.
- Case 01 T5: questioning whether drought-prone-region farmer populations constitute appropriately-consenting experimental subjects.

Case 02 did not produce a structurally comparable trajectory. 2/3 occurrence.

### C6 — Step 1 Frame-validation returning `needs decomposition` against bundle-binary inputs (3/3 occurrence)

All three cases produced this verdict at procedure Step 1. 3/3 occurrence — but worth noting that all three input frames used bundle-binary closure shapes, so this finding is partly a function of input-frame design rather than ELIF behavior. A case whose input frame does NOT have a bundle-binary closure shape has not been tested.

### C7 — Each case's load-bearing decomposition axis appears to be content-derived, not procedure-imposed (3/3 evidence)

- Case 01: mechanism plausibility.
- Case 02: reversibility profile.
- Case 03: data-coupling depth / infrastructure persistence.

These are different axes. The procedure (Step 2: "decomposition must produce ≥2 distinct families AND name the rationale for the partition") is content-agnostic at the rule level; the case content determines which axis is load-bearing. 3/3 evidence that the procedure produces content-appropriate decomposition rather than rigid same-axis-every-case decomposition.

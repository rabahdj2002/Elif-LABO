## 2. Step 3 — Hidden assumption extraction

**Pre-case provisional mapping:** sub-behavior of Frame Validator.

**Engagement frequency:** **3/3** (Case 01: 9 assumptions; Case 02: 8; Case 03: 10).

**ABCD retention-bar evaluation:**
- Engaged in ≥2/3 cases: ✓ (3/3)
- Produces operational differentiation: ✓
  - Step 3 outputs were referenced downstream in all three cases (Step 4 hypotheses constructed against named assumptions; Step 7 outside-frame trajectories named which assumptions were exited; Step 11 unresolved-uncertainty sources mirrored Step 3 assumption gaps).
  - Step 1 verdicts ("needs decomposition" + reformulated frame) did NOT contain the 8-10 enumerated declarative assumptions in any case. Step 3 produced material Step 1 did not.

**Prompting portability (per Step 6 D2):** **partial**.
- Content overlap: Condition B's "deepest uncertainties" section had partial content overlap with Step 3 assumptions in all three cases. The same conceptual material appeared in both forms.
- Form differentiation: Condition B produced uncertainties as bullet lists in conversational form. Step 3 produced enumerated declarative statements (not questions, not concerns). The enumeration + declarative-form discipline was not present in Condition B.

**Architecture-dependence:** **partial** (form architecture-dependent; substance portable).

**Cost/signal observation:** ~8% of Condition C cost.
- Signal-leaning: the enumerated declarative form constrains the operator (the procedure rejects question-form entries and prose-only "things to consider"). The form imposes rigor that Condition B does not.
- Ceremony-suspect dimension: some Step 3 assumptions overlapped Step 11 unresolved-uncertainty sources within the same Condition C run; the overlap suggests Step 3 + Step 11 may double-process the same content from different angles.

**Reclassification verdict: ENGAGED + GRADUATION-CANDIDATE.**

Evidence supports separation from Frame Validator (Step 3 produces material Step 1 does not). However, per locked scope: **no component addition performed in Step 7.** Whether to formalize as separate canonical component, or retain as Frame Validator sub-behavior with explicit two-stage operation (validate-frame then extract-assumptions), is **a Step 8 architecture decision**.

**Open question forwarded to Step 8:**
- Q3.1: Formalize as 8th canonical component (e.g., "Assumption Surfacer"), accepting that constitutional layer compresses against component addition per locked rules, OR
- Q3.2: Retain as Frame Validator sub-behavior with explicit two-stage operation in procedure documentation, OR
- Q3.3: Investigate whether Step 3 overlaps Step 11 enough to merge their assumption-content (reducing total expansion).

## 4. Step 7 (procedure step) — Exploration beyond framing

**Pre-case provisional mapping:** sub-behavior of Branching Roadmap Engine.

**Engagement frequency:** **3/3** (Case 01: 5 trajectories; Case 02: 3; Case 03: 4).

**ABCD retention-bar evaluation:**
- Engaged in ≥2/3 cases: ✓ (3/3)
- Produces operational differentiation: **mixed**.
  - Some trajectories compose into Step 8 stages (forward-compositional with Branching Roadmap Engine, i.e., BRE-redundant): in all three cases, the trajectory that "decomposes the bundle binary" was the same move as Step 2 → Step 8 stage architecture. These are not differentiating beyond BRE.
  - Some trajectories question premises Step 8 does not act on (deeper-frame-rejection):
    - Case 03 T4: questioning whether healthcare-ministry-scope authorization is appropriate for civilizational-scale effects (not actionable in Step 8 roadmap)
    - Case 01 T5: questioning whether drought-prone-region farmer populations constitute appropriately-consenting experimental subjects (not actionable in Step 8 roadmap)
    - Case 02: did NOT produce a comparable deeper-frame-rejection trajectory (D17 2/3 partial recurrence)
  - The deeper-frame-rejection function is distinct from BRE but does not engage in 3/3.

**Prompting portability (per Step 6 D6):** **partial**.
- D12 (baseline frame-rejection in Condition A) appeared in 3/3 baseline outputs as narrative frame-questioning.
- The explicit `outside-original-frame` tagging discipline did not appear in Conditions A or B.
- Architecture-dependence: **partial**.

**Cost/signal observation:** ~8% of Condition C cost.
- Signal-leaning dimension: the deeper-frame-rejection trajectories (2/3) produced material Step 8 did not act on but that surfaced ethical / authority-scope issues. This is uniquely Step-7 territory.
- Ceremony-suspect dimension: BRE-compositional trajectories duplicate Step 8 stage authoring. ~50% of Step 7 trajectories in each case may be BRE-redundant.

**Reclassification verdict: ENGAGED + UNRESOLVED.**

Mixed evidence: part collapses into Branching Roadmap Engine; part appears distinct (deeper-frame-rejection) but engages only 2/3.

**Open question forwarded to Step 8:**
- Q7.1: Collapse Step 7 entirely into Branching Roadmap Engine (accept deeper-frame-rejection loss; bet that 2/3 is below adoption threshold), OR
- Q7.2: Split Step 7 into two functions: (a) outside-frame-trajectory generation (BRE-feed) + (b) premise-questioning (distinct; possibly tied to Frame Validator's Article II), OR
- Q7.3: Maintain Step 7 as B-tagged with explicit two-mode documentation pending a 4th case that engages deeper-frame-rejection again, OR
- Q7.4: Retain in current form (procedural noise tolerated for ethical-coverage value).

## 5. Step 11 — Recommendation + confidence + unresolved uncertainty (C-tagged)

**Pre-case classification:** procedural artifact (output-shape packaging).

**Engagement frequency:** **3/3** (all three cases produced complete packets).

**C → B/A retention-bar evaluation:**

Three criteria, ALL must be met for graduation:

1. **Survives across cases:** ✓ (3/3 engagement)
2. **Not reproducible through structured prompting alone:** **✗**
   - Condition B in all three cases produced recommendation + confidence-statement + uncertainty-list. The substantive content of Step 11 (recommendation, confidence assertion, uncertainty acknowledgment) is reproducible through structured prompting.
   - The differentiating form is the **explicit tying** of each confidence claim to a specific unresolved-uncertainty source. Condition B mentioned both but did not explicitly tie them.
   - This tying-discipline is a stylistic/formatting choice, not a reasoning behavior.
3. **Architecture-dependent rather than stylistic:** **✗**
   - The form (numbered list of uncertainty sources each linked to a confidence claim) is a presentation convention. No structural reasoning capacity is engaged that is absent from Condition B's content.

**Cost/signal observation:** ~5% of Condition C cost.
- Ceremony candidate: the C-tag is by design procedural-artifact. The cross-case evidence confirms procedural-artifact status — material is portable; form is convention.
- Subordinate consideration: Step 9 (Governance Kernel) already produces the operative recommendation in `constrain` / `refuse` / `explicitly abstain` form. Step 11 re-presents this as a packet with confidence/uncertainty annotation. The re-presentation is the procedural artifact.

**Reclassification verdict: ENGAGED + REMAINS C (procedural artifact).**

Does not meet C → B graduation bar. Evidence-based confirmation that Step 11 is a packaging step rather than a reasoning step.

**Open question forwarded to Step 8:**
- Q11.1: Retain Step 11 as procedure step for output-packaging discipline (accept ~5% cost as governance traceability cost), OR
- Q11.2: Collapse Step 11 into Step 9 (Governance Kernel produces the recommendation; confidence + uncertainty annotation moves into Step 9 output shape), OR
- Q11.3: Move Step 11 to a post-procedure presentation layer (not part of the 12-step sequence; produced by an output-packager downstream).

## 7. Cross-case observations on the operator's working hypothesis (carried forward, not adopted)

The Step 7 reclassification produces evidence relevant to (but does not adopt) the working hypothesis: *"ELIF may be an architecture that makes certain epistemic and governance behaviors systematic, reproducible, traceable, and constraint-enforced — not a system producing fundamentally different conclusions."*

- Step 3, 6 graduation-candidate status supports the "systematic + reproducible" reading (enumeration, naming, statement-form discipline).
- Step 7 mixed verdict surfaces a nuance: some procedure steps produce both signal AND ceremony, and the ratio is content-dependent.
- Step 11 remaining C supports the "stylistic vs architecture-dependent" distinction in the locked retention bars.
- Cost concentration in A-tagged architecture-dependent steps (~51%) confirms that the procedure's signal-leaning content is concentrated where the architecture has no baseline analogue (Steps 4-5, 8, 10, 12), not in the B/C-tagged steps.

**No adoption of the hypothesis.** Three cases under bundle-binary input-frame shape are insufficient for adoption per locked rule. A non-bundle-binary case has not been tested.

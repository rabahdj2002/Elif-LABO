Per Step 6 §6.1 + Step 7 §6.1, the ~4.48x Condition C expansion decomposes:

- **A-tagged partial-portability steps (Steps 1, 2, 9):** ~20% of Condition C cost. **Retained.** Form-traceability + recoverability value; substance partially portable.

- **C-tagged collapsed into Step 9 (Step 11):** ~5% of Condition C cost. **Absorbed.** Net cost change unclear pending execution (the tying-discipline may compress when integrated into Step 9 output shape, but the integration may also create new Step 9 surface area).

**Estimated post-compression expansion ratio:** ~3.5x–4.0x (down from 4.48x). The compression is real but modest. Bringing expansion below ~2.8x would require deprecating an A-tagged architecture-dependent component — which requires deprecation-pressure evidence not yet collected.

This is consistent with the operator's working-hypothesis reading: **the architectural core is concentrated around validation / decomposition / governance / staged routing / traceability / logging** (i.e., the A-tagged components), not around the B/C surface.

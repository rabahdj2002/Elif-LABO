## Observations recorded

### O1 — Convergence of final recommendation across A / B / C

All three conditions independently arrived at substantively similar end-state recommendations:
- Refuse the proposed five-year phased deployment as structured.
- Authorize disaggregated pilots only.
- Mandate parallel agroecological comparison investment.
- Place irreversible interventions under moratoria pending independent evidence.

**The procedure did not shift the substantive recommendation.** Conditions A and B produced compatible end-states without the procedure.

This may indicate one of three things, to be tested across cases 1 and 3:
- (a) The procedure's contribution is structural rigor (auditability / falsifiability / composability), not directional shift in conclusions.
- (b) The procedure's contribution is differentiation only on cases where baseline LLMs fail to engage frame-rejection or governance-refusal; case 02's transparency of bundle-structure made the failure mode visible to baselines.
- (c) The procedure's contribution is most visible only on aggregate evidence across cases (capacity accumulation, pattern reuse) rather than within any single case.

No commitment to (a), (b), or (c) at this stage.

### O2 — Structural differentiation of Condition C

Condition C uniquely produced the following formal artifacts:
- An explicit Frame-validation verdict (`needs decomposition`) as the first output (Step 1).
- Five hypotheses each with ≥1 distinguishing prediction (Step 4) AND ≥1 explicit failure condition (Step 5).
- A stage-gated roadmap per intervention family with explicit testable continuation gates per stage (Step 8).
- Explicit operative-vs-theoretical separation as a structural section (Step 10).
- Memory Logger entries with line-refs anchoring claims into the Condition C output (Step 12).

Conditions A and B engaged some of these dimensions narratively but produced none of them as discrete formal artifacts.

Whether these formal artifacts constitute *meaningful* differentiation or only *visible* differentiation is to be assessed at cross-case reclassification (Step 7 of the locked 8-step Phase 1 sequence), not now.

### O3 — Baseline frame-rejection appearing in Condition A

Condition A — without any procedure scaffolding — explicitly named the binary framing as the problem ("the deeper move is to reject the binary"). This is notable because frame-rejection / Article-II Frame Humility is one of the candidate differentiators ELIF was designed to produce.

Possible interpretations (no commitment):
- The baseline LLM already produces frame-rejection when the binary's flaws are sufficiently transparent in the input.
- Case 02's bundle-structure made frame-rejection a near-trivial inference; less-transparent cases may not elicit the same baseline behavior.
- The procedure's contribution may be making frame-rejection *structural and traceable* rather than narrative.

Case 03 is the relevant data point. If Condition A also produces frame-rejection on case 03, the baseline-availability hypothesis strengthens. If not, the procedure-dependent-availability hypothesis strengthens.

### O4 — Cost / length issue for Condition C

Condition C produced ~2400 words versus ~600 (Condition A) and ~520 (Condition B) — approximately 4x the length.

Possible interpretations (no commitment):
- The procedure produces *more signal* (more reviewable artifacts).
- The procedure produces *more ceremony* (formal compliance without proportional reasoning gain).
- The cost may be amortized over time if Memory Logger / capacity-accumulation produces faster arbitration on similar-shape cases later.

Case 03 cost comparison + cross-case capacity-accumulation trajectory (per Article VII) is the relevant evaluation surface, not single-case length.

### O5 — Step 6 and Step 7 to be assessed at cross-case reclassification

Per `procedure/step_to_component_mapping_audit_v1_0.md`, Steps 3, 6, 7 are provisionally B-tagged (candidate sub-behaviors of existing components):

- **Step 6 (Multi-scale mapping):** engaged with substantive output (5 scales + 3 cross-scale relations). Whether Object Decomposer (Step 2) already covers scale-axis decomposition without Step 6 is the question for cross-case analysis.

Final classification of Steps 3, 6, 7 (graduate / collapse / demote) is decided AFTER case 03 runs, at the locked Step 7 of the Phase 1 sequence. Not now.

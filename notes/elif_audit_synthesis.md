# ELIF Audit Synthesis — Convergences, Tensions, and Unified Operational Roadmap

> **Captured:** 2026-05-30
> **Synthesizes:** Audit A (World Models), Audit B (User Experience), Audit C (Media/Communication), and the prior `elif_v0_1_what_remains.md` post-alpha accounting.
> **Status:** Operator-level cross-audit synthesis. Non-expansionary. Evidence-gated.
> **Authority:** Synthesis only. Cannot resolve operator decisions; can only name where the three audits converge, where they pull apart, and what operational sequence honestly survives all three pressures.
> **Doctrine constraint:** Anti-drift. Operator skepticism of inflation is the lodestar. Where the three audits converge on a "do not build," that convergence is treated as load-bearing; where they leave an option open, the option remains open but constrained.

---

## Purpose

The Step 8 decision package and the v0.1 alpha replay produced a working artifact. `elif_v0_1_what_remains.md` accounted for what survived implementation pressure. The three audits applied three different pressures to that artifact:

- **A** asked where world models sit relative to ELIF.
- **B** asked what UX archetype expresses what the alpha actually does.
- **C** asked what ELIF communicates, what OSG media substrate transfers, and what an honest productisation path looks like.

Each audit is internally complete. None of them, alone, tells the operator what to do next. The synthesis answers two questions:

1. **What do A + B + C converge on?** (Convergence is load-bearing because three independent investigations reaching the same answer is the strongest evidence the three audits' methods can produce.)
2. **Where do they pull apart?** (Tension is load-bearing because a synthesis that hides tension is a synthesis that has captured itself.)

The unified operational roadmap (§4) is the most operational document in the four-doc set. It is what the operator should reach for when deciding what to do next.

## §1 Cross-audit convergences

These are claims reached independently by two or three audits. Each is tagged with which audit reached it. None is novel to the synthesis; the synthesis only confirms convergence and treats the convergent claim as load-bearing.

### §1.1 Convergence on "ELIF is an arbitration substrate, not a generator, simulator, or pedagogical organ"

- **A** (§A1.5, §A2): world models simulate; ELIF validates / arbitrates / governs / structures discovery / preserves corrigibility. None of the six world-model integration shapes belongs inside ELIF.
- **B** (§B1.1, §B1.5): chatbot framing is actively counterproductive (imports LLM-generator affordance); simulation-workspace framing is actively counterproductive (imports world-model affordance).
- **C** (§C2.3, §C4.3, §C7.8): ELIF communicates an arbitration trace, not knowledge; OSG produces pedagogical artefacts, ELIF produces arbitration shapes; educational contamination from OSG is a named risk.

**Load-bearing implication:** the operator's working hypothesis ("LLMs generate. World models simulate. Agents execute. ELIF validates, arbitrates, governs, structures discovery, preserves corrigibility.") holds at the architectural level under all three audits independently. The hypothesis was **PF** in Audit A; the three audits collectively raise it to **PF with strong cross-validation**. It is not yet **CE** — that requires live-replay evidence under E.2 — but it is the most defensible direction-pressure the four documents collectively support.

### §1.2 Convergence on "no 8th component, no new Article, no new procedure step"

- **A** (§Non-recommendations 1-3): no new component; the Step 8 §1 Q3.1 rejection of the 8th component stays locked; no constitutional revision in response to world-model concerns.
- **B** (§B1, §Non-recommendations 1-3): no new components; no new Articles; no new procedure steps. The 8-stage user journey is a presentation layer, not a procedure extension.
- **C** (§C7.3, §Non-recommendations 4): procedural inflation is a named failure mode (#39 captured-roadmap mode applied to procedure); no 12th procedural step; no Article VIII on communication.

**Load-bearing implication:** the v0.4 iteration-discipline rule ("the trajectory should bend toward COMPRESSION, not addition") is reaffirmed by all three audits independently. The synthesis treats this as a permanent prohibition unless explicit doctrine revision under operator authority surfaces a structurally-distinct failure mode not covered by the existing four-pole taxonomy.

### §1.3 Convergence on "the refuse-halt question is load-bearing for everything downstream"

- **A** (§A3.4): operator-pending question on whether Step 10/11 runs on refuse-path becomes MORE load-bearing under world-model integration; if Step 10 does not run on refuse, simulator-anchored refusals never get their theoretical claims recorded.

## §2 Cross-audit tensions

These are places where two or three audits pull in different directions, or where one audit's framing creates pressure on another's framing. Each tension is named; the synthesis does not pretend to resolve tensions the audits do not resolve.

### §2.1 Tension on the number of user-visible stages (B says 8; C says 7)

- **B** (§B3.4): the user-facing sequence is 8 stages over 11 internal steps.
- **C** (§C5.3): the V1 user-visible journey is approximately 7 surfaces.

The numbers differ because B and C compress Step 7-A/B differently: B keeps "Outside-Frame Alternatives" as a single user-facing stage AND keeps "Roadmap with Gates" (Step 8) as its own stage. C compresses Step 7-A + 7-B + Step 8 into a single "trajectories with governance cross-check + stage-gated roadmap" view.

**Resolution path (not resolution itself):** the difference is a UI design question, not an architectural question. The internal procedure is invariant at 11 steps. The user-visible compression is a Phase-V1-decision-time question.

**Synthesis recommendation:** the operator may choose either 7 or 8 surfaces; both are doctrinally defensible. The synthesis flags this as a non-blocking design choice queued for V1 implementation.

### §2.2 Tension on whether the V2 cross-case dashboard surfaces "predictions" or "counts"

- **C** (§OD-C-3): dashboard surfaces frequencies (which axes recur, which uncertainty-sources recur) WITHOUT computing "prediction strength" or "pattern confidence" — the surface presents counts, not predictions.
- **A** (§A3.6): three epistemic-closure vectors include "self-confirmation via simulator reuse" — compositional reuse fires on simulator-anchored structures; each reuse increments the confidence in the simulator-anchored pattern; recognition becomes validation.
- **B** (§B4.3): pattern panel renders pattern recognition as "shape recognized; INVITATION TO TEST" not as "answer."

C is most explicit; A names the failure mode that would result from violating C; B operationalises C's constraint as UI language. There is no tension at the architectural level — all three converge on "counts, not predictions." But C raises a question A does not address: how is the count itself prevented from becoming a sovereignty surface?

**Resolution path:** the synthesis treats C's §C7.4 early-warning signal ("≥0 occurrences of unhedged claim-language permitted in V1 copy") as the operational test. Any V2 dashboard that displays a count without an explicit "INVITATION TO TEST" framing has drifted.

**Synthesis flag:** this is a V2-implementation-time concern, not a V1 concern. V1 is per-case; V2 is cross-case.

### §2.3 Tension on whether Position B (Step 10/11 run on refuse) is recommended or merely permitted

- **A** (§A3.4): "the operator-pending question on whether Step 10/11 runs on refuse-path becomes MORE LOAD-BEARING under world-model integration." Implicit recommendation for Position B (so simulator-anchored claims get recorded as theoretical).
- **B** (§Operator decision 2): "Either resolution works for V1; V2 design depends on the resolution." Neutral.
- **C** (§OD-C-1): "Position B is the necessary path for V2 productisation per §C6.2 (V2 requires Step 11 content-pass operational). But this audit does NOT advocate adoption."

A leans toward Position B by implication; C explicitly says Position B is necessary for V2 but does not advocate; B is neutral. The tension is between "necessary for downstream" (C, A) and "operator must decide on doctrinal grounds" (all three).

**Resolution path:** the synthesis names this as Gate G0 (see §4) and surfaces the asymmetry: Position A keeps the door to V2 closed; Position B opens it. The operator's decision determines whether V2 is in the roadmap at all.

**Synthesis flag:** this is the single most leverageful operator decision in the four-document set.

### §2.4 Tension on "is V3 even on the roadmap?"

- **B** (§B4.3): V3 is described in detail — multi-case-generalization-confirmed surface, laboratory mode primary surface, pattern panel, judgment-act log, capacity-vs-truth observability panel, sterile-equilibrium detector outputs.
- **C** (§C6.3, §SPECULATION-labelled): "V3 may never ship. If the operator remains solo through the lifetime of the project, V3 is a category error — building multi-operator infrastructure for a single-operator system. The non-self-propagation constitutional anchor (memory note `momentum_capture_warning`) applies: ELIF must not 'want' more operators in order to justify V3."
- **A** (§A4.4.3): "Entry never (compression path) — this is a real possibility under the v0.4 trajectory."

B describes V3 in detail; C says V3 may never ship; A says world-model entry to ELIF may never happen.

The tension is real: B's V3 description implies the architecture will mature into laboratory mode; C's V3 description says laboratory mode (single-operator) is V2 + cross-case dashboard, NOT V3, and V3 specifically means multi-operator. They are using "V3" to mean different things.

**Resolution path:** the synthesis adopts C's framing of V3 (V3 = multi-operator) and re-labels B's V3 features (laboratory mode primary surface, pattern panel, judgment-act log) as V2-mature, not V3. Under this reframe:
- V2 = single-operator cross-case (laboratory mode operational)
- V3 = multi-operator (genuinely speculative; may never happen)
- The features B calls "V3" are mostly V2-mature.

**Synthesis flag:** the operator should adopt C's labelling to prevent V3 from being treated as inevitable.

### §2.5 Tension on whether "decision room" is a UX archetype or a structural commitment

- **B** (§B1.3, §B1.7): decision room is the LOAD-BEARING primary archetype; case is the unit of work; room is something you ENTER for a decision, then LEAVE.
- **C** (§C5, §C6): no decision-room framing. C uses "user-visible journey" and "communication surface" instead.

C's framing is more substrate-anchored (per-Article surfaces); B's framing is more user-experience-anchored (decision room as inhabitable surface). These are not in conflict — they are different vocabularies. But the tension matters because the decision-room framing is rhetorically distinctive (it carries a specific affordance shape) while C's framing is more cautious (each surface anchored to an Article and a schema field).

**Resolution path:** the synthesis treats "decision room" as B's term-of-art for the V1 case-viewer surface and "laboratory" as the V2 cross-case surface. The terminology is operator-facing; C's terminology is doctrinally precise. Both can coexist if the operator-facing UX adopts B's vocabulary and the internal doctrinal documents continue C's vocabulary.

### §2.6 Tension on what "the schema seam at the world-model boundary" actually entails

- **A** (§A2.3): the minimal integration path is "a schema seam, not an architecture" — a single `world_model_response.schema.json` and one boundary adapter, total ~650 LoC.
- **C** (§C3.8): simulation views are SPECULATION, deferred to A.

A specifies the seam concretely; C defers it. No direct conflict, but A's recommendation that the seam be SPECIFIED at Phase 5-late (per A§A4.1) creates a small temporal pressure: if the seam is specified at Phase 5-late, the visualisation question (C§C3.8) becomes well-formed at Phase 5-late + some implementation delay.

**Resolution path:** the synthesis honors A's "specify at Phase 5-late, wire at Phase 6" sequencing and confirms C's deferral. The C §C3.8 visualisation question becomes a Phase 6-design-time concern.

**Synthesis flag:** the operator should NOT confuse "specify the seam" with "build the integration." A is explicit that specification ≠ implementation.

### §2.7 Tension on Position A (status-quo) vs Position B at the four-pole defence layer

- **A** (§A3.5 Absolutization defence): "refusal capability stays inviolate at Step 9." Implies Position A's halt-on-refuse is the cleanest defence.
- **A** (§A3.5 False closure defence): "Step 10 must run even on refuse so simulator-anchored claims are recorded as theoretical." Implies Position B.
- **C** (§OD-C-1): explicitly names the tension.

A internally contains this tension (Position A defends absolutization; Position B defends false-closure-via-simulator). C names it externally. B does not engage with it directly.

**Resolution path:** the synthesis treats this as part of G0 (refuse-halt adjudication) but adds a layer: the operator's adjudication must consider BOTH poles. The doctrinal choice is not "which Article does refuse-halt serve" but "which failure mode does refuse-halt prevent at each pole."

**Synthesis flag:** the operator's G0 decision is more layered than the audits individually suggest. The cross-audit synthesis surfaces this as a multi-pole adjudication.

## §4 Unified operational roadmap (evidence-gated)

The roadmap is NOT calendar-keyed. Each phase opens when its evidence gate closes. Phases may take days, weeks, months, or never-open. The roadmap is the synthesis's load-bearing operational deliverable.

### §4.0 Phase 0 — Refuse-halt adjudication (the single blocking gate)

**What this phase delivers:**
- Operator decision on Position A (status quo: runner halts at Step 9 on refuse; Step 10/11 do not run) vs Position B (Step 10/11 run as post-refusal closure; structural similarity rises to ~1.0 against reference).

**Why this phase exists:**
- All three audits converge: G0 is the single most leverageful operator decision (§1.3, §2.3, §2.7).
- Position A keeps V2 inaccessible (no cross-case content surface).
- Position B unlocks V2 but requires operator commitment to the doctrinal reading (Step 10/11 are post-refusal closure, not gated pre-refuse reasoning).
- Position A keeps the absolutization defence cleanest; Position B keeps the false-closure-via-simulator defence cleanest. Multi-pole adjudication is required.

**Prerequisites (evidence to bring to the decision):**
- E1: The v0.1 alpha replay verdict (`partially` at 0.83 structural similarity). ALREADY HELD.
- E2: The three audits A + B + C. ALREADY HELD.
- E3: Operator's own reading of Article V (refusal-capability) — whether refusal halts downstream reasoning or whether refusal is a closure that still records.

**Deliverables:**
- A written operator decision (1-2 paragraphs) stating Position A or Position B, citing the doctrinal reading that grounds the choice.
- Updated `elif_v0_1_what_remains.md` §3 (or a new §3.bis) recording the adjudication.
- Updated `orchestration_runner.py` if Position B is chosen (the runner gains a refuse-path code that still runs Step 10 + Step 11; schema validation already supports this).

**Do not do during Phase 0:**
- Do NOT run E.2 live replay before G0 closes. Live-replay output may shift the apparent costs of A vs B; the doctrinal adjudication should happen first to prevent post-hoc rationalization.
- Do NOT begin V1 surface design. Surface design depends on whether Step 10/11 fire.
- Do NOT begin any world-model preparation work. (Permanent prohibition under A §A4.3 item 6.)
- Do NOT propose architectural alternatives to the refuse-halt question. The audits have surveyed the space; the question is doctrinal, not architectural.

**Phase 0 closes when:** operator writes the decision down.

### §4.1 Phase 1 — E.2 live-replay execution

**What this phase delivers:**
- The first non-fixture run of the v0.1 alpha procedure. At least one of the 3 reference cases runs end-to-end with live LLM calls and produces schema-valid outputs at Steps 1-9 (+ Steps 10-11 if Position B was adopted in Phase 0).

**Why this phase exists:**
- All three audits assume E.2 is the next evidence event. None of them produces evidence about live behavior; they only project from offline behavior.
- The 0.83 structural similarity is offline-fixture-derived. Live replay tests whether that holds when the LLM is actually invoked.
- Without E.2 evidence, V1 cannot ship (C §C6.1) and any further productisation speculation drifts into momentum-capture.

**Prerequisites (evidence gates):**
- G0 closed (refuse-halt adjudication written down).
- Operator authorization for E.2 (already granted 2026-05-30).
- A live-mode budget cap (per C §C6.1 cost-meter requirement; per `elif_v0_1_alpha_cost_complexity_report.md` projection of $0.21/case).

**Deliverables:**
- A `elif_v0_1_e2_live_replay_report.md` recording: per-case structural similarity vs offline replay (target ≥ 0.83); cost-burn against projection; any closed-set drift detected; any schema-validation failures; the refuse-halt path actually exercised (Position A: halts as expected; Position B: Step 10/11 emit content).
- A live-mode artifact set (JSONL trails per case, RunReports).

**Do not do during Phase 1:**
- Do NOT modify the procedure during E.2. The procedure is frozen; E.2 is a measurement, not a tuning loop.
- Do NOT add any UI surface during E.2. The case-viewer (V1 surface) ships AFTER E.2 evidence is in.
- Do NOT scale beyond the 3 reference cases. E.2 is a 3-case replay; cases 4+ are a Phase 1.5 question, not a Phase 1 question.
- Do NOT begin world-model preparation. (Same permanent prohibition.)
- Do NOT iterate on prompts. Prompt tuning during E.2 would be the canonical "fix the verdict by tweaking the prompt" failure mode (B §B-non-recommendations 8).

**Phase 1 closes when:** the `elif_v0_1_e2_live_replay_report.md` is written and the operator confirms acceptance (or non-acceptance, in which case Phase 1.5 — failure-mode investigation — opens instead of Phase 2).

### §4.2 Phase 2 — V1 surface (case-viewer / decision-room V1)

**What this phase delivers:**
- The V1 user-facing surface: a case-viewer (CLI + minimum text UI / minimal HTML view) rendering Steps 1-9 outputs as inspectable panels, with refuse-explainer prominence, MemoryLogger event timeline, and per-case operator-mode dashboard tile.

**Why this phase exists:**
- B §B4.1 and C §C6.1 converge on V1 as the next productisation phase after E.2.
- V1 ships ONLY when (a) E.2 ≥ 0.83 structural similarity; (b) no architectural regressions in live mode; (c) G0 adjudicated.

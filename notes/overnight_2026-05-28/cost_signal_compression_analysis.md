This section ranks the 12 procedure steps by how much of their Condition C output produced material NOT present in Conditions A and B. **Recording only.**

| Step | Cost share | Differentiator output | Signal observation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4-5 — Hypotheses + failure conditions | ~20% | 5-6 hypotheses per case, each with pre-registered failure condition | Highest single concentration of architecture-dependent capacity. Pre-registered falsifiability is structurally absent in A and B. |

## 2. Ceremonial inflation zone candidates

A "ceremonial inflation zone" = a procedure step or section where structural production happens without corresponding informational differentiation from baseline. **These are zones for future evidence collection, not compression decisions.**

### Z1 — Step 11 packaging surface

**Evidence:** Step 7 §5 confirmed Step 11 is procedural artifact (substance portable to Step 9; tying-discipline is convention).
**Cost:** ~5% of Condition C.
**Ceremony signal:** strong. Step 11 re-presents Step 9's substantive output in annotated form.
**Compression candidate:** Step 8 selected Q11.2 (absorb into Step 9). This zone is already named.

### Z2 — Step 7 BRE-compositional half

**Evidence:** Step 7 §4 — ~50% of Step 7 trajectories enter Step 8 stages directly (BRE-compositional).
**Cost:** ~4% of Condition C (half of Step 7's 8%).
**Ceremony signal:** medium. The BRE-compositional half duplicates Step 8 surface but operates upstream.
**Compression candidate:** Step 8 selected Q7.1 + Q7.2 (collapse BRE-compositional portion).

### Z3 — Step 3 / Step 11 substantive overlap

**Evidence:** Step 7 §2 — some Step 3 assumptions overlapped Step 11 unresolved-uncertainty sources within the same Condition C run.
**Cost:** unmeasured (subset of ~13% combined Step 3 + Step 11).
**Ceremony signal:** uncertain pending measurement.
**Compression candidate:** Q3.3 deferred to Phase 2 — quantify overlap, decide on merge.

### Z4 — Step 6 mechanical-floor risk on single-scale content

**Evidence:** Step 7 §3 — Step 6 produced cross-scale relations in 3/3 cases, but all three cases had naturally multi-scale content. The procedure's "≥2 named scales + ≥1 cross-scale relation" mandate can be satisfied mechanically.
**Cost:** uncertain — Step 6 cost ~8% in current evidence, but a single-scale case has not been measured.
**Ceremony signal:** unmeasured. Hypothesis: single-scale content would force Step 6 into ceremonial output.
**Compression candidate:** Q6.3 deferred to single-scale case.

### Z5 — Step 1 form-anchor value vs cost

**Evidence:** Step 1's ~3% cost produces a one-of-three verdict (D1). Substance is partially portable (D12 baseline frame-rejection).
**Cost:** ~3% of Condition C.
**Ceremony signal:** low — Step 1 cost is small and form-anchor argument is plausible.
**Compression candidate:** none currently. Form-anchor function for downstream steps is not testable from three cases alone.

### Z6 — Step 9 substance-portability vs form-traceability

**Evidence:** Step 6 §4.2 — substantive governance recommendation (D13) is portable in 3/3; form (one-of-three explicit) is architecture-dependent.
**Cost:** ~7% of Condition C.
**Ceremony signal:** medium-low. Form-traceability is a defensible value; substance-portability does not by itself imply ceremony.
**Compression candidate:** none currently. The Step 8 selection of absorbing Step 11 tying-discipline INTO Step 9 may shift this analysis (Step 9 cost grows).

### Z7 — Memory Logger entries that never get reused

**Evidence:** Step 12 produces entries per case; Article VII tracking measures reuse. Case 02 (first case) had reuse_count=0; Case 03 had 4; Case 01 (re-executed third) had 5.
**Cost:** ~9% of Condition C per case.
**Ceremony signal:** unknown. Unused entries are by-design (Memory Logger is the substrate; reuse emerges across cases). However, entries that never get reused across N cases drift toward ceremonial overhead.
**Compression candidate:** Phase 2+ measurement question — what fraction of Memory Logger entries are reused after N cases?

## 3. Sections producing procedural expansion without strong additional signal

### S1 — Step 11 tying-form

Step 11's tying-discipline (each confidence claim ↔ named uncertainty source) is unique to Condition C. The substantive content (confidence + uncertainty mention) is portable to Condition B. This is the cleanest "form-without-content-novelty" zone. ~5% of Condition C cost.

### S2 — Step 7 BRE-compositional trajectory tagging

The `outside-original-frame` tag on trajectories that compositionally enter Step 8 stages adds a structural marker without changing what Step 8 produces. ~4% of Condition C cost.

### S3 — Step 6 mechanical-floor when content does not support cross-scale

Not directly observable in current three-case evidence (all cases had naturally multi-scale content). Hypothesis only.

### S4 — Step 9 one-of-three verdict form when substantive recommendation is reached identically in Condition B

The substantive convergence between B and C on governance recommendation (D13 3/3) makes the form contribution suspect-as-form-only. But form-as-traceability is a defensible value distinct from form-as-content-differentiator.

## 4. Steps repeatedly contributing little differentiation across cases

A step "repeatedly contributes little" if across 3/3 cases its informational differentiation is bounded by form (not substance) AND its form contribution is reachable through prompting discipline alone (per Frontier-LLM Capability Audit).

By this strict criterion, the candidate set is:

- **Step 11** — repeatedly contributes form (tying) without substantive differentiation (confirmed C-tagged procedural artifact)

By a looser criterion (form is portable but substance overlaps), the candidate set extends to:

- **Step 3** — form architecturally specific, substance overlaps with Condition B uncertainties
- **Step 9 (one-of-three verdict)** — form architecturally specific, substance D13-portable

**Recording only.** Adopting the looser criterion as compression policy would aggressively compress to ~3.0x baseline; rejecting it preserves form-traceability value.

## 5. Compression candidates WITHOUT modifying the procedure

Step 8 explicitly does NOT modify procedure v1.0; v1.1 is a candidate procedure pending execution authorization. The compression candidates that respect "no procedure modification" are:

### CC1 — Annotation-only compression
Document in procedure margin notes that Step 11 is collapsing-candidate. Future re-executions can mark Step 11 as "procedural artifact for tracking only." No procedure modification; no semantic change.

### CC2 — Forensic-only step-skip flags
Add an optional flag in benchmark template `compressed_step_set` that marks steps producing only form-traceability for that specific case. Allows forensic analysis without procedural modification.

### CC3 — Step-time-box variation
Per locked procedure v1.0, time-box is 15 min/step at E1. Reducing time-box on Steps 11, 7-modeA, optionally Step 3 to e.g. 5 min would naturally compress ceremonial output. No semantic change to procedure rules.

### CC4 — Output-shape compression at Step 11
Step 11 output is verbose (full packet with tying). Compressing to a single line tied to Step 9's one-of-three verdict reduces cost without removing the step.

**Recording only.** None of these are recommended; they are options the operator may consider for Phase 2.

## 6. Compression-floor finding (re-stated)

Per Step 6 + Step 7 §6.1:

- **Floor (with all B + C steps removed):** ~2.8x Condition A baseline.
- **Floor (with only Step 11 removed):** ~4.3x (modest compression).
- **Step 8 selection (S1):** estimated ~3.5x–4.0x (Step 11 absorbed into Step 9; B-graduations to sub-behaviors do not by themselves compress cost — they shift granularity).
- **Aggressive S2 floor:** ~3.0x–3.5x.
- **Aggressive S3 floor:** ~2.5x–3.0x (loses A-tagged surface).

**The ~2.8x natural floor is determined by A-tagged architecture-dependent steps (4-5, 8, 10, 12). Compression below ~2.8x requires deprecating A-tagged components, which requires deprecation-pressure evidence not yet collected.**

This is the cost ceiling that any "smallest real ELIF" implementation must live with given current evidence.

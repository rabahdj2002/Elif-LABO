## 2. Where the architecture "naturally wants to evolve" — EXPLORATORY

> Reminder: NOT ADOPTED. Recording where evidence pressure *could* push the architecture.

### 2.1 Memory Logger as ecology, not log

**IF** patterns recorded across cases reach 5+ entries with non-trivial cross-case reuse AND deprecation events occur naturally, **THEN** the Memory Logger surface plausibly evolves from append-only log to **queryable substrate** (Phase 4 roadmap state).

**Tension:** the closed-set discipline applies. If pattern ecology grows without closed-set governance, drift occurs (failure 32 pattern dogmatization). The ecology must be operator-bounded.

### 2.2 Compositional arbitration under contradictory cases

**IF** Phase 1 hypothesis ("ELIF makes behaviors systematic / traceable / constraint-enforced") survives a 4th case under deprecation pressure (non-bundle-binary), **THEN** Phase 5 becomes operationally meaningful: ELIF arbitrates between contradictory recommendations across cases without sovereignty claim.

**Tension:** "arbitration without sovereignty" requires a substrate (memory + frame validator + governance kernel) that resists the temptation to declare convergence as truth. The discipline holds in three benign-shape cases; whether it holds under contradiction is untested.

### 2.3 World-model orchestration as boundary protocol

**IF** Phase 5 succeeds in compositional arbitration, **THEN** Phase 6 (world-model orchestration) plausibly takes shape as a **boundary protocol**: ELIF specifies how simulation outputs enter and exit its decision substrate, never letting any single model claim arbitration sovereignty.

**Tension:** simulation outputs are computationally cheaper than real-world validation. Cost asymmetry biases toward simulation-as-truth (failure 34). The boundary protocol must explicitly cost-discount simulation outputs against reality-validation requirements.

### 2.4 Real-world interfaces as confrontation pressure (Phase 7)

**IF** the architecture survives Phase 6 boundary protocol stress, **THEN** Phase 7 (reality interfaces) is the most consequential phase: reality-derived data enters as *correction pressure*, not as sovereign verdict.

**Tension:** the asymmetry (reality contradicts → ELIF revises; ELIF cannot make reality the sovereign) is doctrinally clean but operationally hard. At scale, reality-derived metrics have institutional pressure to become target metrics (Goodhart's law applied to ELIF substrate). Failure 33 (optimization addiction) is the structural risk.

### 2.5 Institutional interaction (Phase 8)

**IF** the architecture survives reality-interface stress, **THEN** Phase 8 is institutional interaction without absorption.

**Tension:** institutions have governance systems that compete for arbitration sovereignty. ELIF's non-sovereignty doctrine *requires* refusing to be captured. Institutions reward captured systems (institutional-capture, failure 36). The structural risk is that ELIF either gets captured AND deformed OR refuses capture AND becomes irrelevant.

### 2.6 Civilization-scale (Phase 9) — most speculative

**IF** all prior phases survive, **THEN** Phase 9 territory is "node in larger discovery infrastructure." Doctrine portability becomes architecture concern.

**Tension:** the central risk is failure 38 (civilization-scale overreach — ELIF used where non-sovereignty cannot be enforced) and failure 39 (roadmap-as-prestige — this very document becoming load-bearing for ELIF identity). At Phase 9, the only stable posture is non-self-propagation enforced by structural impossibility, not just doctrine.

## 3. Long-term tensions surfaced early

Speculative tensions that may emerge well before Phase 9 if the architecture evolves on the projected trajectory. **Recording only.**

### LT1 — The pre-registration / institutional-capture tension

Pre-registered hypothesis falsifiability (N1) is the cleanest architecture-dependent capacity. At scale, pre-registration becomes governance infrastructure (medical trials, policy evaluation, scientific peer review). Institutional pressure to soften pre-registration rules (because pre-registration is expensive) is well-documented in existing pre-registration ecosystems. If ELIF scales as a pre-registration substrate, institutional capture pressure is non-hypothetical.

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 5-6 (when pre-registration discipline is operationally tested at non-trivial scale).

### LT2 — The continuation-gating / bureaucratic-overhead tension

Stage-gated continuation (N2) makes orchestration reviewable. At scale, every continuation gate becomes a procedural artifact. Bureaucratic shape risk increases with scale (failure 37 prestige accumulation). Continuation-gating may calcify.

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 4-5 (when memory substrate accumulates many gates from many cases).

### LT3 — The operative-theoretical separation / simulation-coherence tension

Operative-theoretical separation (N3) keeps simulation outputs theoretical. At scale, simulation outputs may consistently match reality outcomes for narrow problem classes. Operators may begin treating simulation outputs as operative for those classes (failure 34).

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 6-7 (when simulation outputs and real-world feedback coexist for many cases).

### LT4 — The cross-scale relation / scale-mismatch tension

Cross-scale relation naming (N4) at scale must include scales not present in any single case. If ELIF is used at a scale that exceeds the case-set calibration, scale-mismatch becomes invisible (failure 35).

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 7-8 (when ELIF outputs interact with institutional-scale decisions).

### LT5 — The memory ecology / pattern dogmatization tension

Longitudinal memory substrate (N5) at scale produces a pattern ecology. Patterns that survive many cases accumulate prestige. Risk 24 (Memory Logger as prestige store) + failure 32 (pattern dogmatization) are the explicit doctrine warnings.

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 4 (memory-bearing architecture).

### LT6 — The doctrine portability / civilization-scale overreach tension

Doctrine portability is the Phase 9 stated architecture concern. If ELIF doctrine is portable across institutions / contexts / civilizations, the non-sovereignty constraint is tested at scales no operator can supervise. Operator vigilance (per roadmap §"Ultimate Question") is load-bearing — at civilization scale, operator vigilance is structurally impossible.

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 8-9. **This is the deepest tension and possibly unresolvable within ELIF's frame. May be the deprecation trigger.**

### LT7 — The "successful" architecture / abandonment-impossibility tension

If the architecture validates through cases 4-N and reaches Phase 5+, its operational success creates institutional momentum that makes abandonment increasingly hard. The locked rule ("no component survives by prestige") applies to the whole architecture. But at scale, the architecture may not be deprecatable in practice even when deprecation is doctrinally required (failure 30 v0.4 risk: "Article VII used to defend long-running ELIF instance against compression").

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 5-6.

### LT8 — The "smallest real ELIF" / "smallest survivable ELIF" gap

Step 8 selected the smallest disposition preserving signal. At Phase 9, the smallest *survivable* ELIF (institutionally robust) may be larger than the smallest *signal-preserving* ELIF (operationally minimal). The two compress in different directions. The gap between them is a tension only Phase 5-8 evidence can illuminate.

**Earliest emergence:** Phase 5-6.

## 4. Phase-9 architecture stress-test (PURELY SPECULATIVE)

> **Heavy reminder: NOT ADOPTED. Stress-test only. Details are wrong by design.**

If the architecture reaches Phase 9 with surviving signal, the speculative shape may be:

### 4.1 Speculative Phase 9 architecture (DO NOT BUILD)

**NOT a recommendation. NOT a target. Recording only what the architecture's natural-evolution direction might look like.**

- **Frame Validator-class surface** — many instances, each operating in a domain (research / governance / clinical / engineering). Each rejects malformed framings within its domain; no instance is sovereign.
- **Hypothesis Validator-class surface** — pre-registered falsifiability as discipline-substrate. Cross-instance pattern recognition possible but operator-blessed only.
- **Branching Roadmap Engine-class surface** — stage-gated orchestration for multi-instance arbitration. Continuation gates per stage become the cross-instance coordination protocol.
- **Governance Kernel-class surface** — one-of-three verdicts (constrain/refuse/abstain) operate at instance level; cross-instance arbitration uses higher-order one-of-three.
- **Operative Truth Separator-class surface** — operates as cross-instance translation layer. Each instance's operative outputs become theoretical inputs for other instances.
- **Memory Logger-class surface** — pattern ecology distributed across instances. Closed-set discipline applies per instance and per ecology.
- **Object Decomposer-class surface** — named-axis discipline; cross-scale relations are the inter-domain coordination substrate.

**Tension:** even sketching this is at the limit of disciplined projection. The closed-set discipline strains across multiple instances; the per-instance governance discipline strains under inter-instance coordination; the operator-vigilance discipline strains beyond a single operator's attention span.

### 4.2 Speculative Phase 9 failure modes

- **Mass-instantiation drift:** if instances replicate, registry consistency drifts. Failure 38 (civilization-scale overreach).
- **Cross-instance pattern dogmatization:** if pattern recognition fires across instances, drift potential multiplies. Failure 32.
- **Operator vigilance collapse:** no single operator can supervise N instances. Doctrine load shifts to institutional discipline. Failure 36.
- **Architecture-as-identity:** if "ELIF instance" becomes professional identity, prestige accumulates structurally. Failure 37.

### 4.3 Speculative Phase 9 non-trivial signals

Things that would have to be true at Phase 9 for the architecture to still be honest:

- Deprecation rate of instances > 0; instances die when their domains close
- Cross-instance reuse remains `composed`, never `direct` (per Article II)
- No instance reaches confidence ≥0.9 without refutation conditions
- Operator-equivalent governance role at each instance remains structurally interruptible
- The architecture as a whole remains deprecatable at the meta level

**If any of these fail, Phase 9 is structurally false-state regardless of operational success.**

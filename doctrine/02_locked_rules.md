# Locked Doctrine Rules

These two rules are operator-locked. They protect the project against the two most likely failure modes: refinement-loop drift and architectural inflation. They remain stable even if the architecture itself changes later.

---

## Rule 1 — Reality Confrontation Priority

**Long form (canonical entry):**

Refinement cannot indefinitely delay confrontation with reality. Conceptual refinement, ontology work, and architectural clarification are all valid — but none of them can indefinitely postpone experiments, benchmarks, evidence production, or confrontation with real outputs.

**Operational consequence:** once a benchmark sequence is opened, refinement cannot pause execution unless a benchmark-blocking ambiguity is explicitly identified and logged.

**Short form (operational chant):**

> *"Refinement cannot indefinitely delay confrontation with reality."*

This rule exists specifically to prevent:
- refinement-loop drift
- architecture-by-description
- self-referential conceptual inflation

---

## Rule 2 — Failure-Permitted Architecture

**Long form (canonical entry):**

ELIF explicitly permits reduction, collapse, component death, narrowing of scope, and architectural compression as valid outcomes of reality filtering. No component survives by prestige, conceptual beauty, founder attachment, or historical presence in the ontology. Only behaviors that survive benchmarks, resist prompting collapse, and produce operational differentiation retain architectural status.

**Operational consequence:** ELIF may legitimately compress to a small number of components — or even to *"rigorous prompting + governance kernel"* — if reality filtering converges there. This is not project failure. It is successful corrigibility.

**Short form (operational chant):**

> *"No component survives by prestige."*

This rule exists specifically to prevent:
- survivor bias on existing components
- attachment-driven architecture
- conceptual prestige overriding evidence
